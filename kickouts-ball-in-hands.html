Phase 01 · Field Players · Warm Up
Field Player Warm-Up
~15 min
Movement Circuit
  • Movement CircuitShort · Coach led
⏱ Keep this short — circuit primes movement patterns and raises body temperature. No more than 5–6 mins before moving to plyos.
Plyo Push Game
  • Plyo Push GameCompetitive · pairs or small groups
📌 Competitive format raises intensity and mirrors the physical contest of a kickout. Players should feel the contact before the game begins.
Ball Skills — Jog to Goal, Kick Off Tee
  • FormatJog to goal → ball on tee → kick → stability-mobility exercise
Stability & Mobility Sequence
  • Lunges — forward, controlled
  • Curtsey lunge — rotational hip load
  • Broad to squat jump — power expression into landing stability
📌 The kick-to-stability sequence mimics the asymmetrical demands of a kickout contest — explosive action followed by landing control. Good prep for the contested jumps in Block 1.
Keep transitions sharp between WU exercises — long gaps drop the temperature before the session starts
Plyo push game intensity should be high — it sets the physical standard for the rest of the session
Curtsey lunge: load the trail hip fully — this is where kickout contest injuries come from

5-5-5 Kickout Practice
  • Structure5 short · 5 mid · 5 long
  • TargetsOther keepers or coaches as movers
  • Short: goalkeeper’s own defensive shape — quick ball to feet
  • Mid: contested zone — ball to a moving target, not a static one
  • Long: aerial delivery, target player calls their line
📌 Targets must be moving when the ball is struck — static target practice doesn’t prepare the keeper for live kickout decisions.

KO Principles
01
Targets
Designated target players must present clearly and call their line before the ball is struck. The keeper hits the target — the target doesn’t chase the ball.
02
Movement
Off-ball players must move to create secondary options. Standing still is a turnover waiting to happen — movement forces the defence to make decisions.
03
Breaks
When the primary contest is lost, players must sprint to the breaking ball. Breaks are neutral possession — the first player there with intent wins them.

KO Defence + Separation Game
~15 min
Full Game — KO Defence Focus
  • FormatFull game · 2 groups · full pitch
  • FocusKickout defensive organisation
🎯 The full game now applies KO defensive structure. Both teams must organise before every kickout. Evaluate whether the principles from Block 1 transfer into a live game context.
SSG — Separation Game
  • Format3rd group · SSG area · coach led
  • RuleEvery re-start stops until man-to-man marking set
  • Tackle ruleYou can only tackle your own man
  • FocusTeam in possession — their movement to create separation
📌 The Separation Game builds the skill on the other side of the kickout contest — getting free of your marker. Every restart waits until man-to-man is fully set, then the challenge begins. The focus is entirely on how the team in possession moves to break the marking structure.
