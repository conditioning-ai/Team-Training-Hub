3 B’s of Support Running
  • Support Running DrillWith ball
  • Behind — runs in behind the last line of defence, creating depth and scoring opportunities
  • Beside — support on the shoulder of the ball carrier, maintaining width and providing an immediate option
  • Before — support in front of the ball carrier, offering the short option and keeping possession moving forward
📌 The 3 B’s of support play directly feed the “Work High Value Shots” principle — players arriving Behind, Beside, and Before the ball carrier create the range of options that lead to high-percentage scoring opportunities.

Phase 02 · Whiteboard · Player-Led
Principle Setting
~8 min
Primary Principle
  • ThemeWork High Value Shots
  • SourceWhiteboard on pitch
📋 The coach puts the question to the players: “What makes a shot high value?” Players describe what they see and feel on the pitch from their own experience. The coach’s job is to listen carefully and find the simplest, clearest language to capture what each player means — not to impose the answer, but to reflect it back in a form the whole group can hold onto and use.

Phase 03 · Half Pitch · 11v11
“Every Attack Matters”
~20 min
Format
  • PitchHalf pitch
  • GamesA v B & C v D (simultaneous)
  • Balls5 balls each game
Progression Structure — 5 Balls
  • Balls 1–3: No constraints. Attackers play freely. Defenders play normally. Read what the attack does naturally.
  • Balls 4–5: Defender constraint. Coach of the defending team sets up a specific defensive shape (man or zone) to challenge the attackers. This is about testing the attack — not developing the defence.
🎯 The constraint is a challenge to the attackers, not a defensive drill. Coach of the defending team directs the set-up. Man and zone are the easiest constraints to apply.
Defensive Constraint Options
  • Man markingEasiest to set
  • Zonal defenceEasiest to set
📌 The defending coach calls the shape for the last 2 balls. Attackers don’t know what’s coming until they receive the ball — keeps the read-and-react quality high.

Phase 05 · Half Pitch · Repeat
Repeat — Every Attack Matters
~15 min
Same Format — Higher Fatigue Context
  • FormatHalf pitch 11v11 — same structure
  • ContextPost full-game fatigue
  • ScoreContinues from earlier
🎯 Return to the half-pitch 11v11 format after the full game and SSG block. Bodies are tired — decision-making under fatigue is the stimulus. The session principle is tested for real here.
Coaching Focus
  • Are the sub-principles from Phase 02 showing up under fatigue?
  • Are attackers still working high-value shots or taking low-percentage options when tired?
  • What decision-making breakdown appears first as fatigue sets in?
  • This is the best read of the session — observe before you intervene.
📋 End of session: go back to the whiteboard. Ask the players whether what they described in Phase 02 actually showed up in the game. Let them evaluate first — the coach’s role here is to confirm, clarify, or gently challenge what they see, not to deliver a verdict.
Minimum coaching intervention here — this is the test, not another teaching moment
Announce the final score at the end. Name what mattered — high value or not.
If one team is running away with it, ask why publicly — let players reflect on shot selection together
