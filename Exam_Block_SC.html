Exam Block S&C

2 Sessions · 1 Optional Restore Day · Maintenance · No Progressions

Coach's Notes

This programme is for field sport athletes who cannot participate in games or contact training during an exam period. The goal is to maintain — not develop. Keep the aerobic engine ticking, speed patterns sharp, and the body strong, without accumulating the fatigue that impairs cognitive performance.

Toggle between sessions across the top. Inside each session, tap any block to expand it — Warm-Up, Prep, Plyos, Strength and so on. Every exercise name links to a YouTube search demo.

Do not train to failure. Do not push beyond prescribed intensities. Do not add volume. The exam block is for staying ready — not for adaptation.

📋 Session A — Coaching Notes

  • Order is non-negotiable: Warm-Up → Prep → Speed → Extensive Plyos → Gym. CNS-demanding work always first.
  • Speed at 80–85% only. Maintenance, not testing. Smooth mechanics beat raw effort during exam block.
  • Extensive plyos = low intensity, high contact quality. Pogos are about ankle stiffness; broad jumps about landing quality.
  • Gym: RIR 3 on everything. Finish each set feeling 3+ reps left in the tank. Never grind to failure.
  • Feeling mentally flat? Cut to Warm-Up + Prep + Speed (~25 min). That's a valid exam-block session.
  • Best days: Monday or Tuesday. Leave at least one full day before Session B.

📋 Session B — Coaching Notes

  • Intensive plyos before conditioning — always. Hurdle hops and depth drops need a fresh CNS. Never after a run.
  • Tempo runs: pick one option. Option 1 (tempo runs) suits field athletes. Option 2 (MAS) needs a measured distance.
  • Conditioning is controlled-hard — not a test. All 6 reps similar. If the last 2 slow, drop to 4 without guilt.
  • The light gym finish is intentionally short. 2 sets after conditioning is maintenance — more risks unnecessary fatigue.
  • Very fatigued after exams? Do Warm-Up + Prep + Plyos and skip conditioning. A reduced session beats no session.
  • Best days: Thursday or Friday. Minimum 2 days after Session A for CNS recovery.

📋 Restore — Coaching Notes

  • When to use: Poor sleep multiple nights, HRV below baseline, high exam stress, or significant soreness.
  • When NOT to use: If you feel normal. This replaces a session on a bad week, or fills a third day if the schedule allows.
  • Alternatives: 40-min hilly walk · light swim or pool walk · gentle yoga. Rule: leave feeling better, never exceed 70% MaxHR.
  • The cardiac output jog should feel genuinely easy — 'embarrassingly slow' is correct. Resist the urge to push.
  • Built on Joel Jamieson's restoration framework — the aerobic system is the primary recovery mechanism. Keep it aerobic.
