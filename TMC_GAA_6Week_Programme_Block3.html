Block 3 Theme
Power and density. Running sessions hit shorter, sharper rep formats with minimal rest — training the ability to repeat high-speed efforts under fatigue. Gym shifts toward heavier compound loading and complex-pair methods.
Running Variation
VO2 day uses 30–15 Intermittent Fitness format and flying sprint sets — maximal speed under accumulated fatigue. Tempo day introduces extended 250m reps and squad-format relay-style work for mental variety.
Gym Variation
All seven patterns — brand-new exercises. Introduces complex pairs (heavy compound → plyometric) for post-activation potentiation. Plyo stays at 3 rounds but complexity increases. Nordic curl volume builds from Block 2.
Progression Logic
Build 1 (Wks 1–2) → Build 2 (Wks 3–4) → Peak (Wks 5–6). Gym loads 5–10% above Block 2 peaks. Running intensity is maintained but rest windows compress — total density is the overload mechanism this block.

Block 3 — What Changed & Why
  • Δ1
    Running Density is the Overload — Not Distance Flying sprints test true maximum velocity — something neither timed intervals nor distance repeats can isolate. Rest-pause 200m sets mirror the work-rate of the fourth quarter of a match. This is the most game-specific conditioning in the programme.
  • Δ2
    Complex Pairs Introduced in the Gym Heavy compound lift paired with an explosive movement exploits post-activation potentiation — the nervous system fires harder in the plyometric after a maximal strength effort. This is the most powerful gym method for athletic power development.
  • Δ3
    All Gym Exercises Rotated Again — All 14 New Three blocks, zero repeated strength exercises across any of the seven movement patterns. Your body has had to adapt to a completely different mechanical demand every six weeks. That breadth of exposure is what builds a robust athlete.
  • Δ4
    Nordic Curl Volume Increases to 5 Reps Block 2 built the base at 4 reps. Five reps shifts this from an activation stimulus toward a genuine strength stimulus. Still full rest — still maximal quality. Never reduce rest to fit the session; reduce reps instead.
  • Δ5
    Gym Loads: 5–10% Above Block 2 Peaks Three blocks of progressive overload stacked. If you have logged weights consistently across all three blocks, you should be able to demonstrate significant strength gains in every pattern. That is the entire point of periodisation.
After three blocks, retest everything — MAS, 30-15 IFT level, back squat 5RM, and broad jump distance. The numbers don't lie.
If you completed all three blocks with consistent loading, you are a meaningfully different athlete from where you started Block 1
Block 4 options: repeat Block 1 at higher loads as a deload-and-rebuild, or continue to a transition / maintenance phase ahead of competition
