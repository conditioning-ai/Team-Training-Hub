⚡ In-Season Volume Protocol
This program is the in-season version of the Full Body Level 1 session running at 70–75% of off-season volume. Plyometrics reduced from 5 exercises (3 sets each) to 3 exercises (2 sets each). Power block reduced from 4×4 to 3×3. Tri-sets maintained across all 3 blocks but reduced from 3 sets to 2 sets per tri-set. RAMP warm-up and special circuits are unchanged — non-negotiable in-season. Intent on all main lifts stays maximal. Load may need slight reduction to match reduced fatigue tolerance during competition weeks.

In-season adjustment: Jump Squat reduced from 4×4 to 3×3. Med Ball Chest Pass reduced from 3×5 to 2×4. Hang Power Clean remains optional for coached athletes at 2×3 (was 3×3). Intent is always maximal — the bar must feel fast every rep. If movement quality drops, terminate the set.

In-Season Power Block Jump Squat: BW or empty bar in-season — speed of movement is the stimulus, not load. If legs are fatigued from match/training, reduce to BW only and still go at maximal intent. Med Ball: drive from hips first — this is not an arm press. HPC: two clean sets at full intent is more valuable than three grinding sets when fatigued.
In-season adjustment: All three tri-sets are retained but reduced to 2 sets each (was 3). This preserves the movement patterns and stimulus while reducing total volume by ~33%. Exercises, tempo, and loading targets are unchanged. Load by feel — RIR on all exercises; if legs are heavy from a match, reduce load by 10–15% and maintain RIR targets. Rest 90s after completing all three exercises in each tri-set.

In-Season Tri-Set Guidelines 2 sets per tri-set is sufficient to maintain strength in-season — do not add a third set to compensate. Back Squat: 55–65% 1RM, concentric always powerful. RDL: feel the hamstring tension; no lumbar rounding. Reverse Nordic: 30–45° range is correct at Level 1 — quality eccentric over range. Copenhagen: short lever in-season; progress to long lever when off-season returns. If playing a match within 48 hours, reduce load by ~10% and raise RIR by 1 on all main lifts.
Instructions: Each athlete selects one circuit at the end of each session that addresses their current injury concern or limitation. If no issues are present, choose Hip Mobility or Ankle Mobility as a maintenance option. These circuits are unchanged in-season — managing injury risk is even more critical during competition phase. Perform 2–3 rounds with minimal rest.
