Variety Key Variable
Block 2 Goal
Continuation from Block 1 — volume and intensity rise modestly, but variety is the primary adaptation driver. New stimuli, new angles, same rigorous structure.
Running Variation
VO2 day shifts from pure time intervals to distance-based and shuttle formats. Tempo day introduces intensive tempo sets and broken-run formats. Both formats are new to your system.
Gym Variation
All movement patterns retained — Squat, Hinge, Push, Pull, Rotate, Brace, Land/Jump — but every exercise is different. Heavier loading where appropriate. New plyo circuits with reactive and unilateral emphasis.
Progression Logic
Same 3-cycle structure: Accumulation (Wks 1–2) → Intensification (Wks 3–4) → Realisation (Wks 5–6). Loads should be 5–10% heavier than peak of Block 1 from Week 1 onwards.

Block 2 — What Changed & Why
  • Δ1
    Running Format Changed — Not Volume Distance-based and shuttle formats create a different adaptation signal to time intervals. Same energy systems, different mechanical and perceptual demands. Variety is the overload.
  • Δ2
    Plyo Circuits: 2 rounds → 3 rounds The single biggest volume increase in this block. Plyo quality — not the strength work — is your injury risk lever. Back off a round immediately if landings lose control.
  • Δ3
    All Gym Exercises Rotated Same seven movement patterns — every single exercise is new. This drives neural adaptation and prevents accommodation. You'll feel weaker initially — that's the variety working.
  • Δ4
    Nordic Curls Introduced The most evidence-backed exercise for hamstring injury prevention in field sports. Low reps, full rest, maximal intent on the eccentric. Do not rush or fatigue this exercise.
  • Δ5
    Gym Loads: 5–10% Heavier Across the Board Block 1 built the movement quality. Block 2 adds load. If you're unsure of your Block 1 peaks, pick weights where you could do 2 more reps at the end of each set — then add from there.
Feeling more tired in Week 1 of this block vs Week 1 of Block 1 is expected and correct
If sessions consistently run over 60 min on gym days, reduce rest between sets — not exercise selection
RAMP warm-up v2 is non-negotiable — the carioca and A-skip sequence prime mechanics for the new run formats
