Day 1 focus: Lower body plyometric qualities — vertical stiffness, horizontal power, and lateral elasticity. Volume is slightly higher than the single-session version (4 sets on key exercises) as this is the dedicated lower plyo day. All jumps: soft landings, knees tracking toes, no valgus on landing.

Day 1 Plyo IntentQuality of landing is the priority at Level 1. If knee valgus, groin sag, or trunk collapse appears on landing, strip back and cue. Total ground contacts ~90–100 for this session. Med Ball work moves to Day 2.
Coaching intent: Day 1 power work targets lower body force-velocity — Jump Squat for vertical power, Med Ball Scoop for hip extension drive. Jump Squat volume increased to 5 sets (from 4) as this is the dedicated lower day. Bar must feel fast every rep — if it doesn't, the load is too heavy.

Power Block – Loading GuidanceJump Squat: BW or empty bar to start. The purpose is speed of movement — load only if the bar feels light and bar speed is maintained. Scoop Throw: this is a hip hinge to an explosive jump, not a bicep curl — the arms follow the hips.
Structure: 3 lower-dominant tri-sets. Tri-A pairs the main squat pattern with the primary hinge — both at 4 sets to take advantage of the dedicated lower day. Tri-B is single limb + hamstring prehab stack. Tri-C is lower accessories and core. Move directly between exercises; rest 90–120s after each tri-set round. Load by feel — RIR on all work.

Day 1 Tri-Set Loading GuidelinesBack Squat: 55–65% 1RM approx., concentric must feel powerful. RDL: feel the hamstring load at the bottom — no lumbar rounding. Bulgarian: 3-second eccentric — don't rush the descent. Reverse Nordic: 30–45° lowering range is fine at Level 1. Hip Thrust: full glute contraction at lockout every rep.
Instructions: Each athlete selects one circuit at the end of each session. If no active issue, choose Hip Mobility or Ankle Mobility. Perform 2–3 rounds with minimal rest.

Day 2 focus: Upper body reactive work and full-chain med ball plyometrics. Box Jump is included at reduced volume for lower body maintenance. Shock Push-Ups introduce upper body reactive loading. Med Ball work is the primary plyo stimulus for this session.

Day 2 Plyo IntentMed Ball work is the priority plyo stimulus today. Quality of throw matters — explosive hip rotation and extension drives the ball, not arm strength. Shock Push-Up: land on hands with some give, immediately push back up — the transition from landing to push is the stimulus.
Coaching intent: Day 2 power block focuses on upper body rate of force development and full-chain linkage. Med Ball Chest Pass is the primary upper body ballistic. Hang Power Clean remains an optional progression for coached athletes. SA Med Ball Slam adds rotational and anti-lateral flexion demand.

Power Block – Day 2 LoadingChest Pass: stand 1–1.5 m from wall, explosive push from chest — do not lob. SA Slam: reach full overhead extension before slamming, tighten core on the way down. HPC (optional): prioritise catch height and full hip extension — never sacrifice these for load.
Structure: 3 upper-dominant tri-sets. Tri-A is a push + pull superset with scapular prehab. Tri-B is a vertical pull paired with overhead pressing and posterior shoulder work. Tri-C reintroduces a single limb lower (step-up) plus anti-rotation core and muscular endurance plank. Load by feel — RIR throughout.

Day 2 Tri-Set Loading GuidelinesBench: controlled 3-second eccentric, explosive push — no bouncing off the chest. Row: lead with elbow, squeeze scapulae at the top. Chin-Up: full hang at the bottom, chin clears bar at top. Overhead Press: no lumbar extension — brace core before pressing. RKC Plank: posterior pelvic tilt, squeeze glutes and quads, pull elbows toward feet — this is not a rest position.
Instructions: Each athlete selects one circuit. If no active issue, choose Shoulder Immobility as a maintenance priority on Day 2, or Hip Mobility. Perform 2–3 rounds with minimal rest.
