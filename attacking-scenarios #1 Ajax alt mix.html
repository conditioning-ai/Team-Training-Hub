Approach — Differential Learning
  • MethodDifferential Learning
  • Pattern“Up, Back, Through”
💡 Differential Learning deliberately varies the movement stimulus rep-to-rep. Players self-organise their technique rather than repeating a fixed pattern — builds robust, adaptable skill.
Drill Progressions
  • Stage 1 — Basic cone drill: player runs the up-back-through pattern solo through cones. Establish the movement shape.
  • Stage 2 — Coach give & go: coach varies the return ball (hand, kick, height, weight) on every rep. Player must read and adapt.
  • Stage 3 — 1 skill + 4 steps to shoot: player receives, takes 1 skill (solo, hop, or catch) and has exactly 4 steps to get a shot away.
🎯 The 4-step shooting rule forces decisive, clean footwork from the moment of reception — no dithering, no extra solo.
Coach: vary the return ball deliberately — different height, weight, and type every single rep
Players should feel slightly uncomfortable — that’s the differential learning working
The 4-step rule is strict — count it out if needed. Footwork quality under constraint is the whole point.
Don’t over-coach technique — let players self-correct across reps

Session Structure
  • Setup3-lane pitch for Ajax & att scenarios
  • Ajax constraints3:1s ×2, with tempo
  • FormatAjax game interweaved with 4 att scenarios
📌 The Ajax game runs “between the lines” as the base game. Each attacking scenario is inserted between Ajax rounds — same pitch, different problem each time.
Session Sequence
Setup
Ajax Game — Constraints 3:1s ×2
Establish the game. 3:1 overloads, with tempo runs between rounds. Players get the feel of the pitch and lanes.
Att Scenario #1
Score in Middle Lane — 15 Seconds
Attack must penetrate through the middle lane and score inside 15 seconds from initiation. Forces direct, decisive attacking play.
Ajax Game
Between the Lines
Return to the Ajax game. Players apply what they found in Scenario #1 back into open play.
Att Scenario #2
Wide Attack — Random Line to Line
Wide attack initiated from a random lane. Attack must transition through lanes to create the scoring opportunity.
Ajax Game
Between the Lines
Back to open play. Wide attacking movement from Scenario #2 should now appear naturally in the Ajax game.
Att Scenario #3
Wide Attack — “Up, Back, Through”
Connects directly to Phase 03 technical work. Wide attack uses the up-back-through pattern to penetrate. Players now apply the drill pattern under game pressure.
Ajax Game
Between the Lines
Open play again. The full wide attacking toolkit — Scenarios #2 and #3 — now available to players in the game context.
Att Scenario #4
Over, Through or Around — Exploration
Open-ended attacking problem. No prescribed solution — attack must decide whether to go over (kick), through (penetrate middle), or around (wide). Player-led decision-making under live conditions.
The Ajax game is the vehicle — the attacking scenarios are the lesson. Let the game breathe between scenarios.
Scenario #4 is deliberately open — resist the urge to tell them what to do. Observe the decisions they make.
The 15-second rule in Scenario #1 is strict — reset the clock visibly so everyone knows when time is up
Scenario #3 should echo the technical work from Phase 03 — if the pattern isn’t showing up, prompt it once and let them run it
