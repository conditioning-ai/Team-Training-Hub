Programme Goal
Build field sport readiness through progressive aerobic-speed development. Two dedicated running sessions and two full-body strength sessions per week build every quality needed for game day.
Structure
Three 2-week cycles — Foundation (1:1), Transition (2:1), Peak (3:1) — with progressive overload built in across all sessions. Volume and density increase each cycle.
Running Days
Day 1: VO2 Max intervals — high intensity, strict work:rest. Day 2: Tempo runs — sub-maximal, rhythmical, mechanically sound. Every running session opens with a 12-minute RAMP warm-up.
Gym Days
Day A (Tue) and Day B (Fri). Each session opens with an extensive plyo circuit, then works through Squat, Hinge, Push, Pull, Rotate, Brace and Land/Jump. Log your weights every session.

Game Principles — Non-Negotiables
  • P1
    Progressive Overload Volume and density increase every 2 weeks. This is deliberate. Each new cycle is harder than the last — trust the process and do not skip ahead.
  • P2
    Form First If running mechanics break down or lifting form deteriorates — stop the set immediately. Fatigued poor movement creates injury, not adaptation.
  • P3
    Active Rest Only Between run intervals, walk or light jog. Never stand static. Active rest keeps the aerobic system engaged — it is part of the protocol.
  • P4
    Context Management In-season: reduce run volume to the lower rep range. Match week: shift sessions earlier. Pre-season: execute full programme as written.
  • P5
    Retest Protocol Complete a 6-Minute Modified Cooper Test before Week 1 and after Week 6. MAS = Distance ÷ 100. Target improvement: 1–2 km/h over the block.
Skills and tactical sessions are managed by your coaching staff — integrate around them
